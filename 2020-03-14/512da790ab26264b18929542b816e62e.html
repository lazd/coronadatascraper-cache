Frequently Asked Questions about COVID-19

  • Risk of infection to public in Shasta County is believed to be low.
  • According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, older adults (ages 60 and higher) as well as people who have serious, chronic medical conditions (like heart disease, diabetes and lung disease) are at higher risk for contracting COVID-19. These individuals should avoid crowds as much as possible as well as cruise travel and non-essential air travel.
  • All residents should practice good health hygiene which includes washing your hands, staying home if you are sick, avoiding close contact with people who are sick and covering your cough or sneeze with a tissue or your sleeve (not hands).

Shasta County HHSA provides accurate and timely information to the public. Our Public Health Laboratory processes specimens on suspected COVID-19 cases. Testing for COVID-19 is also available through commercial labs, and healthcare providers may order testing for ill patients directly through a commercial lab. As per disease reporting protocols, commercial labs are required to report positive test results to Public Health officials. Shasta County HHSA reports positive COVID-19 cases to the public.
