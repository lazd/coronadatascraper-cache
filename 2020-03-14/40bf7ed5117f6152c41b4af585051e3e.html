Situation in Fresno County

There is no immediate threat to the general public in Fresno County. The Fresno County Department of Public Health (FCDPH) has plans in place to inform and protect all residents, limit exposures to any new cases if identified, and address concerns as they arise.

On March 14, 2020, the FCDPH confirmed the second case of coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) in Fresno County. The FCDPH is working closely with the California Department of Public Health (CDPH) and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) to monitor COVID-19.

This is a rapidly evolving situation and information is being updated as it becomes available.

Español

Fresno County residents, students, workers, and visitors should continue to practice good public health hygiene as this is the height of flu season. The Fresno County Department of Public Health (FCDPH) reminds you there are a number of steps you can take to protect your health and those around you:

  • Wash hands with soap and water
  • Avoid touching eyes, nose or mouth with unwashed hands
  • Avoid close contact with people who are sick
  • Stay away from work, school or other people if you become sick with respiratory symptoms like fever and cough.
